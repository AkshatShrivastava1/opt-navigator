## What is Optional Practical Training?

Optional practical training is one type of work permission available for eligible F-1 students. It allows students to get real-world work experience related to their field of study.

While a Designated School Official (DSO) recommends OPT in SEVIS, it is the student who must apply for the work permit with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). If the OPT is approved, USCIS will issue an Employment Authorization Document (EAD). The student must not begin working before the start date on the EAD.

## OPT Types

There are three general types of OPT:

- OPT: For students engaged in post-secondary schools or at conservatories. Twelve months of regular OPT is available for each higher level of study (for example, a student may have 12 months for a bachelor's degree and another 12 months for a master's degree).
  - Pre-Completion OPT: Any portion of OPT used before the student's Program End Date. It may be part-time or full-time.
  - Post-Completion OPT: Any portion of OPT used after the student's Program End Date. It must be at least 20 hours per week or full-time.
- 24-month (STEM) OPT Extension: For students who majored in designated Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) degrees approved by DHS. This type of OPT is a 24-month extension of OPT.
- Cap-Gap OPT Extension: For students whose prospective employers filed a qualifying H-1B-cap subject petition.

## OPT Request Statuses (in SEVIS)

- Requested: DSO requested OPT in SEVIS on behalf of the student, but USCIS has not yet received the Form I-765.
- Pending: USCIS has received the Form I-765.
- Approved: USCIS has approved the Form I-765.
- Denied: USCIS has denied the Form I-765.
- Canceled: DSO or a scheduled SEVIS maintenance job canceled the practical training request in SEVIS.
- Withdrawn: USCIS approved the student's request to withdraw the Form I-765.

## OPT Process and Key Filing Deadlines

Remind F-1 students:
- Not to file the Form I-765 until the DSO has recommended OPT in their SEVIS record and they have the updated Form I-20 with the OPT recommendation.
- They must file within the 30-day period after the DSO OPT recommendation.

If a student submits the Form I-765 (on paper or electronically) before the DSO recommends the OPT, or after the 30-day post-DSO-recommendation period:
- USCIS will deny the application (federal regulations require the DSO OPT recommendation on the student SEVIS record before the Form I-765 is submitted).
- The student will lose the application fee and will have to reapply and pay the fee again.

Form I-765 Filing Deadlines Once DSO Requests OPT in SEVIS:
- Day 1: DSO requests OPT in SEVIS; 30-day countdown begins.
- Day 30: 30-day countdown ends; USCIS must receive the application.
- After Day 30: Applications received by USCIS after day 30 will be denied.

The Form I-765 is available on the USCIS website at http://www.uscis.gov/i-765.

Any F-1 Optional Practical Training request in Requested status longer than 180 days is automatically canceled by SEVIS.

Post-Completion OPT: Start Date must be on or after the Program End Date. It cannot be more than 60 days after the Program End Date. End Date cannot be more than 12 months after the Employment Start Date.

## Reporting While on OPT

While on OPT, a student is required to report the following to their DSO:
- Change in student's legal name.
- Change in student's address while on OPT.
- Changes in employment or interruption of employment.

Students can enter employment information using the SEVP Portal, or DSOs can update SEVIS to reflect the changes. Employment information must be updated in a timely manner. While on OPT, the student must report to the DSO within 10 days any changes in the student's name or address, and employment information. The DSO must update SEVIS within 21 days with the employment information and any name or address changes.

## Unemployment While on OPT

While students are not required to have an employer when OPT is requested in SEVIS, students authorized for post-completion OPT are expected to be employed. A student on post-completion OPT can be unemployed for a total of 90 days.

- A student whose record lacks employer information is considered unemployed.
- SEVP officials can manually terminate a student who accrues 90 total days of unemployment.

## Cap-Gap (Automatic Extension in SEVIS)

SEVIS will automatically extend post-completion OPT or the 24-month STEM extension of OPT if:
- On or after April 1, an employer files an eligible H-1B petition to change the student's status.
- The requested start date for the H-1B is October 1.
- The student's OPT (or 17-month OPT extension) ends on or after April 1.

## Qualifying Employment Note

CPT counts toward OPT eligibility: Full-time CPT at a previous school at the same program level counts toward the CPT 12-month total affecting OPT. CPT at another program level does not affect OPT.
