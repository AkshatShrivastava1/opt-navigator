## What Form I-765 is for

Certain aliens in the United States may file Form I-765, Application for Employment Authorization, to request an Employment Authorization Document (EAD). F-1 students apply for OPT work authorization by filing Form I-765 (after the DSO enters the OPT recommendation in SEVIS). You may file online by creating a USCIS account, or file a paper application.

After USCIS approves a Form I-765, the EAD card should be produced within 2 weeks and is mailed via USPS Priority Mail. Allow a total of 30 days from approval before inquiring with USCIS.

## OPT eligibility categories (Question 27 on Form I-765)

- (c)(3)(A) — Pre-completion OPT: File Form I-765 up to 90 days before being enrolled for one full academic year, provided that the period of employment will not start before you have completed one full academic year.
- (c)(3)(B) — Post-completion OPT: File Form I-765 up to 90 days before, but no later than 60 days after, your program end date. You must file your Form I-765 within 30 days of the date that your DSO enters the recommendation for OPT into your SEVIS record.
- (c)(3)(C) — STEM OPT 24-month extension: File Form I-765 up to 90 days before the expiration of your current OPT, if you are requesting a 24-month STEM extension.

## Where to file

The filing address depends on your reason for applying and the eligibility category you entered in Question 27. Check the filing locations for Form I-765 for the list of addresses.

## Fees

The filing fee for Form I-765 is listed on the USCIS Fee Schedule page. The fee depends on the filing category and filing method (online vs. paper). Confirm the current fee on the USCIS Fee Schedule before filing.

## Filing tips

- Don't forget to sign your form. USCIS will reject any unsigned form.
- E-Notification: To receive an email/text that USCIS accepted your form at a lockbox, complete Form G-1145 and clip it to the first page of your form.
- If your mailing address changes after you file, update your address with USCIS and USPS promptly, or your case could be delayed or your document lost.
