# OPT Employment Types and Reporting — DHS Study in the States (SEVIS Help Hub)
Source: https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-help-hub/student-records/fm-student-employment/f-1-add-edit-delete-optional-practical
Publisher: DHS / SEVP
Last updated: September 5, 2024

## Reporting requirements while on OPT

While on any type of OPT, a student is required to report: a change in the student's legal name; a change in the student's physical, mailing, or employer address; and a change in or interruption of employment. Students on post-completion OPT (including the STEM extension) can report certain personal address and employment changes using their SEVP Portal accounts. Anything the student cannot or does not report via the SEVP Portal must be reported to the DSO within 10 days; DSOs have 21 days to update SEVIS.

OPT employment must be directly related to the student's major area of study; it is the student's responsibility to explain that relationship.

## Types of OPT employment situations (post-completion OPT)

A student may have different employment situations on post-completion OPT:

- Multiple employers: A student may work for more than one employer. Repeat the add-employer process to add a second or a third employer (edit the existing employer's end date, then add the new employer).
- Self-employed / independent contractor: A student may be self-employed on post-completion OPT. If self-employed, SEVIS populates the employer name with the student's name; the student enters the primary location where the work is performed (a residence, office, or formal business). NOTE: Students on the STEM OPT extension cannot be self-employed.
- One employer with multiple sites: Allowed; the student reports the employment location that best meets reporting responsibilities.
- Multiple short-term gigs in one period: Allowed; the student indicates they are self-employed and describes the work (for example, playing music or acting with multiple gigs) in the Employment Remarks.
- Volunteer / unpaid: Allowed; enter the name and address of the organization where the student volunteers and note in Employment Remarks that the student is a volunteer.
- Unemployed: If the student has no employer, nothing is entered. A record that lacks current employer information counts as unemployment.

For the STEM OPT extension specifically, the employer must have an Employer Identification Number (EIN) and provide supervisor details, and the student cannot be self-employed or a volunteer (STEM OPT requires a paid training opportunity with an E-Verify employer).

## Employer dates and unemployment

All employer start and end dates must fall within the actual OPT-approved period of work authorization. Employer dates outside that range affect the calculation of the student's days of unemployment. SEVIS may terminate a student's record if the student exceeds 90 days of unemployment during post-completion OPT, or 150 days across the post-completion and STEM periods.

## Correcting employer data

DSOs can add, edit, or delete OPT employer information for up to 60 days following the OPT actual end date. Changes that cannot be made through SEVIS require a Help Ticket (data fix) via the SEVP Response Center.
