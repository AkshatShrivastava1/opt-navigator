### Q3. When can I apply for post-completion OPT?

You can apply for post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) under the following conditions:

- You must apply no earlier than 90 days prior to your program end date. [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5]
- You must apply no later than 60 days after your program end date. [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5]
- You must file a properly completed application no more than 30 days after your Designated School Official (DSO) enters the OPT recommendation into your SEVIS record. [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5]

### Q5. How long is the grace period after my OPT ends?

An F-1 student who has completed an authorized period of post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) may remain in F-1 status for 60 days beyond the Employment Authorization Document (EAD) expiration date. This is commonly known as a grace period, during which the F-1 student may prepare for departure from the United States or take other actions such as contacting their Designated School Official (DSO) to change their education level or transfer to another SEVP-certified school [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].

### Q6. How long is the STEM OPT extension, and who's eligible?

The STEM OPT (Optional Practical Training) extension is 24 months long. To qualify for this extension, you must:

- Have been granted OPT and currently be in a valid period of post-completion OPT [source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-extension-for-stem-students-stem-opt].
- Have earned a bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree from a school that is accredited by a U.S. Department of Education-recognized accrediting agency and is certified by the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) at the time you submit your STEM OPT extension application [source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-extension-for-stem-students-stem-opt].
- Be an F-1 student who received a STEM degree included on the STEM Designated Degree Program List [source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students].
- Be employed by an employer who is enrolled in and is using E-Verify [source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students].
- Have received an initial grant of post-completion OPT employment authorization based on your STEM degree [source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students].

### Q9. Can I travel internationally while on OPT?

Yes, you can travel internationally while on Optional Practical Training (OPT) if you meet certain conditions. To re-enter the United States to engage in OPT, you must have:

- A current visa (if applicable) [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-7].
- A Form I-20 endorsed for travel by your Designated School Official (DSO) within the last 6 months [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-7].
- An unexpired Employment Authorization Document (Form I-765 / EAD) [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-7].

Keep in mind that time spent outside of the United States does not extend your period of authorized OPT and counts towards the maximum allowed period of unemployment [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-7].

### Q10. What is cap-gap?

Cap-gap refers to the period during which an F-1 student who is the beneficiary of a cap-subject H-1B petition is granted an extension of their F-1 status and employment authorization. This extension occurs automatically if the following conditions are met:

- The F-1 student is currently authorized for Optional Practical Training (OPT) employment [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].
- The F-1 student has not violated the terms and conditions of their nonimmigrant status [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].
- The H-1B petition was timely filed [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].
- The H-1B petition requested an H-1B employment start date of October 1 of the following fiscal year [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].

The cap-gap period starts when the F-1 student's status and employment authorization expire and ends on October 1, the start date of the H-1B cap-subject petition filed on their behalf, unless terminated [source: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-2-part-f-chapter-5].

### Q11. Can I do volunteer or unpaid work to avoid unemployment days?

Yes, you can do volunteer or unpaid work to avoid unemployment days, as long as the employment is related to your program of study and does not violate any labor laws. You must enter the name and address of the organization where you volunteer and note in the Employment Remarks that you are a volunteer [source: https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-help-hub/student-records/fm-student-employment/f-1-add-edit-delete-optional-practical]. 

However, for the STEM OPT extension specifically, you cannot be self-employed or a volunteer; you must have a paid training opportunity with an E-Verify employer [source: https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-help-hub/student-records/fm-student-employment/f-1-add-edit-delete-optional-practical].

### Q12. Is self-employment allowed on OPT?

- However, students on the STEM OPT extension cannot be self-employed [source: https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevis-help-hub/student-records/fm-student-employment/f-1-add-edit-delete-optional-practical].

### Q13. What happens if my program ends while my OPT application is still pending?

If your program ends while your Optional Practical Training (OPT) application is still pending, your F-1 status will remain valid as long as you comply with all requirements for maintaining your student status.
